What is BMR?

Your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the energy (kcal) your body uses at complete rest to keep you alive—breathing, circulation, cell repair, etc. It does not include activity.

Why it matters
  • Sets your maintenance baseline: add activity to estimate total daily needs.
  • Useful for planning weight loss/gain targets.
Terms on this page
  • Sex: used by the Mifflin–St Jeor formula for BMR.
  • Age: older age usually lowers BMR.
  • Height (cm), Weight (kg): body size changes resting energy needs.
  • Activity factor: multiplies BMR to estimate TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure).
  • TDEE: estimated maintenance calories per day.
Formula (Mifflin–St Jeor)
Male: BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5
Female: BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age − 161
What the chart shows

Bars compare your BMR (resting) vs TDEE (BMR × activity). TDEE is what you’d eat to maintain weight at your chosen activity level.
