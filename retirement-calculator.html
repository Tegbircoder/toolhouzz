Retirement Calculator

Project your nest egg with monthly contribution, growth, and inflation — in both nominal and today’s dollars.

This free retirement calculator helps you estimate how much your savings could grow by the time you stop working. Enter your current age, your target retirement age, what you’ve already saved, and how much you plan to invest each month. Then choose a long‑term annual return and an inflation rate. We’ll chart two lines side‑by‑side: the nominal amount (raw dollars in the future) and the value in today’s dollars after accounting for inflation. Seeing both makes it easier to compare your future balance with today’s prices.

Use this tool for high‑level planning of RRSP/TFSA contributions, workplace pensions, and personal investments in Canada. Try changing the monthly contribution or return to see how sensitive your outcome is. This is an educational model—not advice—and real returns vary. For personalized guidance, consider speaking with a qualified advisor.

How to read the results
  • Nominal: future dollars without inflation adjustment.
  • Today’s CAD: nominal divided by cumulative inflation.
  • Tip: increase monthly contribution, or test 5–8% return with 2–3% inflation for long‑run scenarios.

Methodology

We assume monthly investing with compounding. Let PV = current savings, PMT = monthly contribution, r = annual return (decimal), i = annual inflation (decimal), and Y = years until retirement.

  • Monthly rate: r_m = r / 12
  • Months: N = 12 × Y
  • Future value (nominal): FV = PV·(1+r_m)N + PMT · ((1+r_m)N − 1)/r_m
  • Inflation‑adjusted FV (today’s CAD): FV_real = FV / (1+i)Y

This is a simplified projection and ignores taxes, fees, withdrawal patterns, and sequence‑of‑returns risk.

FAQ

What return and inflation should I use?
For long‑run planning, many people test 5–8% annual returns with 2–3% inflation, but markets are uncertain. Try a range to see sensitivity.
Does this include RRSP/TFSA tax effects?
No—this is a growth model only. Tax treatment can change outcomes. Consider separate tax planning or professional advice.
Is the result guaranteed?
No. Returns vary year‑to‑year. This tool is for education, not financial advice.
How can I reach a target amount?
Increase monthly contributions, extend the time horizon, or test higher expected returns (with risk in mind). Use the chart to compare scenarios.
